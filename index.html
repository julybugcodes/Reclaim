RECLAIM Privacy Policy

Effective date: 19 July 2026

The short version

Almost everything in RECLAIM lives only on your own device. There are no ads, nothing is sold, and there is no analytics or tracking of your behaviour. A small, optional set of features (Connect, Following, and your Feed's publish/share options) send limited data to a shared backend so those features can work between devices. Sections below explain exactly what, and the real limits of how that data is protected.

1. What stays on your device only

The large majority of RECLAIM — your companion, trackers, journal entries, gratitude jar, memories, letters you haven't sent, trips, to-dos, kitchen inventory, and settings — is stored using your browser's local extension storage. This data is never transmitted anywhere by RECLAIM. It stays on the device you're using unless you export a backup yourself.

2. What the optional social features send to a shared backend

If you use Connect, Following, or choose to publish to your profile in Feed, the following is sent to and stored in a shared Firebase database used by all RECLAIM users:

  • A randomly generated device ID and a friend code — neither is your name or email unless you choose to make your display name identifying
  • Messages and letters you send through Connect, and their contents
  • Who you follow and who follows you
  • Anything you explicitly mark to publish to your profile (travel stamps, poster walls, photos, videos, short text posts)

Photos and videos you choose to publish are uploaded to Firebase Storage, a separate file-hosting service, and become accessible via an unguessable but not access-controlled link.

Important limitation, stated plainly

This backend does not use user accounts, passwords, or authentication. It relies on friend codes and device IDs being hard to guess, not on cryptographic access control. Anyone who obtained your friend code, or gained access to the underlying database directly, could potentially read data associated with it. This is an appropriate model for a small trusted group of friends. It is not appropriate for a large public launch without adding real authentication first, and you should not rely on it to protect anything sensitive.

3. Third-party services RECLAIM talks to

Various features fetch data from external, independent services when you use them. Each is contacted only for the specific feature that needs it, RECLAIM does not run any of these services itself:

  • Wikipedia / Wikimedia (facts, photos, article summaries)
  • iTunes Search API and Open Library (book and film cover art)
  • iNaturalist and GBIF (wildlife identification and sightings)
  • Jamendo (free licensed music streaming)
  • TomTom (traffic estimates for Leave By)
  • Open-Meteo and postcodes.io (weather and location lookup)
  • 17track.net (parcel tracking, opened as a link, not embedded)

Each of these has its own privacy policy governing what happens to requests sent to it. RECLAIM does not control or audit their practices. Some of these services are based outside the UK, so using those features involves your request data (not your RECLAIM account data) being sent to servers in other countries as a normal part of how the internet works.

4. No advertising, no selling data

RECLAIM does not display ads, does not include any advertising or analytics SDKs, and does not sell or rent data to third parties, ever.

5. Children's privacy

RECLAIM is not intended for use by anyone under 13 years old. RECLAIM does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. On first use, everyone is asked to confirm they are 13 or older before the app can be used at all. If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information to RECLAIM, contact us using the details below and it will be deleted.

6. Your control over your data

  • Export: Settings → Back up your data, downloads everything as one file
  • Delete everything: Settings → Start fresh, erases all local data immediately
  • Unpublish: republishing an empty selection, or turning off profile sharing, removes future visibility
  • Unfollow / block: available from the Following page at any time
  • Backend deletion request: contact us using the details below

7. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, we'll update the effective date at the top.
